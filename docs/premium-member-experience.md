# Premium Member Experience

## Home philosophy
Home answers one question: **what should I do today?** It prioritizes a greeting, the current day, membership context, today's workout, Atlas guidance, today's journal, weekly progress, and recent activity only when data exists.

## Navigation philosophy
The primary navigation is intentionally small: Home, Workouts, Journal, Atlas, and Menu. The Menu drawer contains secondary destinations while developer pages remain under `/dev`.

## Journal philosophy
Nutrition is renamed Journal because members think in terms of a daily fitness diary. Journal groups meals, meal photos, water, sleep, recovery, measurements, progress photos, daily notes, and timeline.

## Atlas philosophy
Atlas is positioned as a coach, not a diagnostic page. It appears on Home, in the bottom bar, and through floating access with conversational guidance and no developer metadata.

## Workout philosophy
Workouts should feel browsable and premium with today's workout, library discovery, programs, training splits, creation, history, favorites, recently used items, search, filters, imagery, difficulty, duration, and exercise count.

## Pricing philosophy
Pricing is public, polished, and plan-oriented for Consumer, Trainer, and Gym customers with clear comparison cards, feature highlights, FAQ, CTAs, and enterprise contact language.

## Empty-state philosophy
Empty states never invent fake values. Each state includes an icon, headline, helpful description, and a single CTA that moves the member forward.

## Product direction
GymCord should feel simple, premium, modern, and focused: less clutter, more guidance, less developer language, and more consumer clarity. Advanced analytics and diagnostics belong behind `/dev`, not in production navigation.
